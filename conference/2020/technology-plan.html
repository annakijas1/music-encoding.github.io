Technology Plan for Virtual Conference

This page will be updated in the days leading up to the event. Questions can be directed to music-encoding2020 at conftool.net.

The 2020 Music Encoding Conference will be run as a virtual event.

We will use Zoom and Slack for conference attendance. All presenters and registered attendees will receive details about joining via Zoom and Slack in advance of the event, and are welcome to join the Zoom meeting to follow the Keynotes, Paper “Slam” Sessions, and Town Hall meetings. Both the discussions following the Paper “Slam” Sessions, and the Interest Group meetings will be held via Slack.

In order to foster discussion and questions, presenters’ papers will be made available for attendees to read about a week prior to the event. We encourage presenters and attendees alike to join the Humanities Commons Music Encoding Initiative group, where presenters’ papers will be deposited and accessible.

In addition to these platforms, we encourage community backchannel conversation – before, during, and after the conference – on Slack and Twitter (#MEC2020).

Using Zoom

All registered attendees are welcome to join the Zoom meeting. You do not have to have a Zoom account or client on your computer. You can join the meeting in your browser. Details will be provided to all participants via email shortly before the event.

  • How to join a Zoom meeting
  • Getting started with Zoom

After the keynote presentation, there will be time for a few questions from the audience. A discussion period will be held in Slack following the Paper “Slam” sessions.

General Zoom Guidelines for Participants during the Conference

  • Please keep your microphone muted at all times.
  • You can turn off your video camera to save bandwidth.
  • If you have a question during a workshop, please use the raise your hand function or the chat function. The host or moderator will keep track of the questions and call on people to unmute, if they wish to ask their question.
  • Questions for the keynote speakers will be held until the end of the presentation. Please use the raise your hand function or the chat function. The host or moderator will keep track of the questions and call on people to unmute, if they wish to ask their question.
  • Questions during the Paper Slam sessions will be held until the end and can be asked during the discussion group in an assigned Slack channel on the Music Encoding Initiative Slack (music-encoding.slack.com).
